Fun Facts About Vincent Van Gogh

  • Birth and Death: Born on March 30, 1853, in the Netherlands, and died on July 29, 1890, in France.
  • Artistic Career: Van Gogh only began painting seriously in his late 20s, producing over 2,100 artworks in just over a decade.
  • Famous Works: His most notable pieces include "Starry Night," "Sunflowers," and "The Bedroom."
  • Post-Impressionism: He is known as a key figure in the Post-Impressionist movement, characterized by bold colors and emotional expression.
  • Mental Health: Van Gogh struggled with mental health issues throughout his life, which influenced his art.
  • Self-Portraits: He created around 30 self-portraits, exploring his identity and emotions.
  • Influence: His work has had a lasting impact on modern art, inspiring countless artists and movements.
  • Letter Writing: Van Gogh exchanged over 600 letters with his brother Theo, providing insights into his thoughts and artistic vision.
  • Recognition: He was largely unrecognized during his lifetime, selling only one painting while alive, but is now celebrated as one of the greatest artists in history.
  • Legacy: The Van Gogh Museum in Amsterdam houses the largest collection of his works and continues to attract millions of visitors each year.
